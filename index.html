Meet Mitra

Mitra Khazai has served Madison Elementary School students, families, and taxpayers as a Board Member since 2015. Mitra has lived in the Madison School District since 1996 with her husband, Greg and three sons. Her deep and wide experiences in the district as a parent, community leader, volunteer, taxpayer and governing board member have informed her views on public education.

Our Message

“Taxpayers, parents and teachers know that meaningful experience matters as schools balance a variety of opinions, challenges, and opportunities. I would very much appreciate continuing my impactful and seasoned work on the Madison Governing Board for another four years.” - Mitra Khazai

First Hand Understanding
Mitra's children attended Madison neighborhood public schools, a charter school and an out-of-district public school. She has navigated the world of special education with Section 504 Plans and an Individual Education Plan (IEP). Her two oldest sons attend Arizona public universities and she has witnessed how our public school system prepared them for the rigors of college. These experiences have given Mitra a powerful first-hand education into the challenges parents face as they navigate a rather complicated educational system.
Future Economic Growth is Dependent on Strong Schools
Mitra believes public schools play an important role in achieving and reflecting our American ideals. She serves on the governing board because she believes how students are educated today will substantially determine the kind of society we all live in tomorrow. After attracting new businesses to Arizona, Mitra knows first-hand that employers invest in communities that have a well educated workforce. Our society profits from better informed citizens and a highly skilled workforce.
Road Ahead
Looking forward Mitra will be focused on teacher and staff retention and recruitment, building on Madison’s high academic outcomes and helping every student reach their full potential. This must be done while we provide a safe environment to learn that supports social and emotional development and health for both students and staff, along with consistent oversight so our taxpayer-provided resources are well invested.

Priorities

Ensuring students, parents and taxpayers have a voice on the school board.
Investing in employees so Madison continues to attract and retain talented educators.
Supporting student and staff social and emotional health.
Maintaining Madison's high educational standards and expectations.
Providing reliable oversight so taxpayer-provided resources are used effectively.
Ensuring a safe environment to learn and teach.
Experience
  • Chair, Madison Site-Based Leadership Team
  • President, Madison Governing Board
  • Co-Chair, Madison Advocates
  • President, Windsor Square Special Conservation District
  • Member, Phoenix Light Rail Advisory Committee
  • Appointed Member, Arizona Board of Education A-F School Accountability Ad Hoc Committee
  • Appointed Member, Parent Advisory Council, Arizona Department of Education
  • PreK-12 Certified Arizona Substitute Teacher

Mitra graduated with a B.A. in Political Science from Northwestern University and has worked in state government policy, environmental and economic development fields in three states. In Arizona, she worked at the Arizona Department of Commerce in business development under three different governors, ran the Southwest Public Recycling Association and is a founder of Family Promise - Greater Phoenix, which provides emergency shelter to homeless families while moving them to self-sufficiency. She currently serves as the Executive Director of the Grammy award-winning Phoenix Boys Choir.
